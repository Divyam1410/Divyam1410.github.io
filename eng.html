Art of Tamil Nadu
Thanjavur painting is a classical South Indian painting style, which was inaugurated from the town of Thanjavur in Tamil Nadu. Thanjavur paintings are panel paintings done on wooden planks, and hence are referred to as Palagai Padam in local parlance. In modern times , these paintings have become souvenirs for festive occasions in South India- colorful pieces of art to decorate wall, and collectors' items for art lovers, as also sadly sometimes, dime-a-dozen bric-a-bracs to be purchased from street corner practitioners.




Dance Style Of Tamil Nadu
Bharatnatyam is a dance of Tamil Nadu in southern India. It traces its origins back to the Natyashastra, an ancient treatise on theatre written by the mythic priest Bharata. Originally a temple dance for women, Bharatnatyam often is used to express Hindu religious stories and devotions.It was not commonly seen on the public stage until the 20th century. The dance movements are characterized by bent legs, while feet keeping rhythm. Hands may be used in a series of mudras, or symbolic hand gestures, to tell a story.




What Women wear in Tamil Nadu
Sari found a special place among their costumes, saree, blouse and half saree is the traditional dress for women of Tamil Nadu.Pavada or half-saree is another traditional outfit for Tamil Nadu especially worn by girls before getting married.Pavada symbolises the young age and beauty of Tamil girls.




What Men wear in Tamil Nadu
The traditional dress of men look as elegant as women's. Generally, they use Lungi and angavastram. The traditional Lungies were invented in South India. Lungi is the most common part of men's outfit.

Jewellery
Tamil people have historically been connoisseurs of fine golden jewellery, which has a history predating the Sangam period in the Indian subcontinent. Ancient Tamil literature lists out the different types of jewellery worn by women historically from head to toe. Apart from gold, jewellery was also fashioned out of silver, copper and brass.





Music In Tamil Nadu
Music of Tamil Nadu has a long tradition and history going back thousands of years. Music is a very important aspect of marriage and temple festival Tamil people.The tradition of Tamil music goes back to the earliest period of Tamil history. Many poems of the Sangam literature, the classical Tamil literature of the early common era, were set to music.Music was also utilised in the compositions of the Tamil Saiva saints during the Hindu revival period between the sixth and the tenth centuries CE.
